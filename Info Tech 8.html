Online Teen Safety Guide

Essential internet safety tips to help teens navigate the virtual world.

1. Protect Your Hardware

Your devices hold access to your emails, personal info, and vital passwords. Keep them physically and digitally secure:

  • Set Up Passwords: Insist on password protection for all computers, tablets, and phones.
  • Turn on Locators: Enable native locator functions (like Find My iPhone or PC equivalents) to track lost devices or remotely wipe data if they fall into the wrong hands.
  • Physical Security: Use computer cable locks in public places like libraries or coffee shops.
  • Secure Sensitive Info: Never leave raw lists of passwords unattended. Any files storing sensitive information should be password protected.

2. Defend Against Malware & Viruses

Malicious software can secretly access your computer and steal information anytime you connect to the internet.

  • Use Anti-Virus Software: Ensure you have robust anti-virus software installed and keep it updated.
  • Beware of Downloads: Do not download software or utilities from non-reputable vendors, as they can be Trojan horses hiding malware.
  • Watch for Symptoms: Take action immediately if a browser starts acting weird or floods the screen with pop-up windows.

3. Mobile Privacy & Identity Theft

Teens are just as susceptible to identity theft as adults. A ruined credit rating early on can make building a decent credit history an uphill battle.

  • Limit Location Services: Do not allow apps to use your location services by default, as it may reveal where you live.
  • Check Your Photos: Be careful sharing photos with landmarks that could give away your home address over social networks.
  • Keep Identifying Info Private: Never share your exact birthdate or home address online. Scammers can use innocent-looking online quizzes to steal security question answers.

4. Scams and Online Shopping

Online shopping presents unique dangers, from cheap knock-offs to hidden fees.

  • Use Reputable Platforms: Only make sales or purchases through well-known platforms like Amazon or eBay, which offer purchase protection.
  • Check App Subscriptions: Review app marketplaces regularly to ensure you aren't paying weekly subscription fees for apps you forgot about.
  • Limit Spending: Use allowance features, gift cards, or prepaid cash cards to limit a teen's access to unlimited credit online.

5. Cyberbullying & Online Gaming

Wherever teens congregate online, there is a possibility for hurtful or inappropriate behavior.

  • Watch for Warning Signs: Sudden secrecy, avoiding devices, dismay after reading messages, or signs of depression can indicate a teen is facing cyberbullying.
  • Gaming Risks: Online gaming communities can involve trash-talking that escalates to bullying, and gaming itself can sometimes turn into an addiction.
  • Open Communication: Always maintain open communication without "freaking out." Document the abuse and be ready to report it to authorities—cyberbullying is illegal in many jurisdictions.
